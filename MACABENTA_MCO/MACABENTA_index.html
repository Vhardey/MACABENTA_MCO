HIP-HOP DANCE

"Hip-hop speaks when words can't—move, feel, repeat."

ABOUT HIP-HOP

Hip-hop dance is a powerful way to express emotion and individuality through rhythm and movement.
It turns every beat into a story, connecting dancers to music and culture.

HISTORY

Hip-hop dance began in the 1970s in the streets of New York, created by Black and Latino youth who used
movement to express their culture and struggles. It grew from breaking, popping, and locking into a global
art form that continues to evolve and influence dance worldwide.

CULTURAL IMPORTANCE

Hip-hop dance has shaped fashion, music, and youth culture by giving people a bold way to express identity and
attitude. It brought communities together, spread street culture across the world, and inspired new creative
movements in art, media, and entertainment.

KEY ASPECTS OF HIP-HOP DANCE

Elements of Hip-Hop Culture

Hip-hop isn't just dance, it includes DJing, MCing, graffiti art, and knowledge of the culture, all working together to create a full artistic movement.

Battles

Dancers often face each other in friendly battles to show skill, confidence, and creativity.

Freestyle

Freestyling lets dancers improvise and create on the spot, showing raw creativity and personal style without any choreography.
